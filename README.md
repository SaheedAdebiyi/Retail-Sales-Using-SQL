# Retail Sales Performance & Profitability Investigation Using-SQL
**Author:**  Saheed Olayinka Adebiyi
**Date:** 2025-12-18
---
## Project Background
This comprehensive analysis addresses six critical business questions raised by leadership regarding retail sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, and operational efficiency. The report provides data-driven insights to guide strategic decision-making and resource allocation. 
---
## Project Objective
**The following objectives focus on improving operations, marketing, and customer satisfaction by analyzing sales and customer data**
1.  Identify whether our revenue growth is translating into real profit or being eroded by high costs
2.  Analyze the categories with high sales volume that also deliver strong profitability
3.  Study which customers drive the most revenue and repeat purchases.
4.  Examine and tailor promotions and product offerings to the right demographic groups.
5.  To determine the sales trend over time
6.  To determine whether customers typically make bulk purchases or small, frequent ones.

---
## Datasets
- **supermarket_sales_2025.xlsx** — The data consists of 2,000 rows and 16 columns, which is clean without duplicates and empty spaces, except for creating new conditional columns
Dataset main columns include: transaction_id, customer_id, gender, age, category, quantity, price_per_unit, total_sale, cogs, total_cost, sale_date, sale_time

---
## Key Findings (example)
1.  Established clear investment priorities based on profit margins and transaction volumes
2.  Segmented customers by lifetime value and identified top revenue generators for retention programs
3.  Revealed distinct purchasing patterns across age groups and genders for targeted marketing
4.  Identified peak sales periods for optimized staffing and inventory management
5.  Analyzed quantity patterns to inform inventory planning and promotional strategies

---
## Recommendations
1. Increase marketing spend on high-margin, high-volume categories
2. Develop win-back campaigns for one-time purchasers
3. Implement personalized recommendations based on purchase history
4. Adjust staffing levels to match peak sales periods by day
5. Optimize inventory stocking based on seasonal and monthly trends
6. Schedule promotions during slower periods to balance revenue throughout the year
7. Implement bulk discount strategies to increase average basket size
